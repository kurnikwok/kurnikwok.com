Work-burden shift

Work-burden shift is the before/after signal that more recurring work burden has moved from the responsible person to shaped intelligence.

Definition

Work-burden shift is the visible before/after change where more recurring work burden moves from the responsible person to shaped intelligence while judgement, approval and real-world action remain human-owned.

In plain English: The test is whether the person has less recurring work to rebuild, carry and restart, while still owning the decisions.

Why this term is needed

The relevant question is not whether an AI output is impressive. It is whether the operating pattern changes the amount of recurring burden the person must keep carrying.

Before and after example

Before, the responsible person carries the recurring reconstruction burden. After, shaped intelligence carries more of that continuity into reviewable form while responsibility remains human-owned.

How it differs from adjacent ideas

Adjacent ideaDistinction
Responsibility shiftWork-burden shift is not responsibility shift. Human judgement, approval, escalation, communication and real-world action remain human-owned.
Productivity claimA productivity claim may be broad or vague. Work-burden shift asks what burden moved, what remained human-owned and what evidence supports that view.
Reproducibility proofPublic evidence can support initial capability evaluation, but protected-stage review is required before stronger method-transfer claims.

Example

Before: the person repeatedly reconstructs context, status, risks, dependencies and next actions. After: shaped intelligence carries much of that continuity into review-ready form for the person to direct and approve.

Non-example

A faster draft that still requires the person to recreate the work basis and review logic from scratch.

Assessment signal

The visible burden areas include reconstruction, sequencing, drafting, tracking, dependency and risk framing, domain-practice application, state preservation, resumption and communication preparation.
