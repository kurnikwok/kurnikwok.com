Concept page

Work-burden shift shows what AI shaping changes

Work-burden shift is the before/after signal that shaped intelligence is carrying two sides of recurring burden: domain-practice and subject-context burden, and work-state and resumption burden. In practice, that means less rebuilding by the responsible person while humans keep judgement, approval, communication, escalation and real-world action.

Recognise the problem

The friction is the work before the AI output can be used

Work-burden shift is easiest to see when the person is not merely asking AI for a better answer, but repeatedly rebuilding two kinds of conditions: the domain-practice and subject-context side of the work, and the work-state and resumption side that preserves state and keeps work resumable.

Reader route: use this page for the burden-shift mechanism, then use the capability evidence paper when evidence review is needed.

Do you restate the background and source basis every time?
Do you re-apply domain-practice standards before AI can help?
Do you re-explain subject-specific context and reasoning?
Do you recreate unresolved matters, review status and next action?
Do you rebuild the resumption point after every pause?

Before and after, compact

Before, the person keeps rebuilding domain fit, subject context, work state and the resumption point for the AI. After, shaped intelligence carries more of that reusable work basis, so the person directs, reviews and decides instead of starting again.

Definition

Work-burden shift is the visible before/after change where more recurring work burden moves from the responsible person to shaped intelligence while judgement, approval and real-world action remain human-owned.

The shift is two-sided: it covers domain-practice and subject-context burden, and work-state and resumption burden. The component terms used on this site are domain-practice standards, subject-context reasoning, work-state preservation and work-resumption logic.

In plain English: the test is whether the person has less recurring work to rebuild, carry, apply and restart.

The burden shifts because the shaped work pattern carries more of the reusable work basis while the person still owns the decisions.

Why this term is needed

The relevant question is not whether an AI output is impressive. It is whether the operating pattern changes how much domain-practice and subject-context burden, and how much work-state and resumption burden, the person must keep carrying before AI output can become usable work.

Two sides of the shift

SideComponent termsWhat shaped intelligence carries
Domain-practice and subject-context burdenDomain-practice standardsSubject-context reasoningRelevant practice expectations, subject-specific context and interpretation fitted to the work.
Work-state and resumption burdenWork-state preservationWork-resumption logicSource basis, unresolved matters, review state, next action and the logic needed to resume without rebuilding the work basis.

How it differs from adjacent ideas

Adjacent ideaDistinction
Responsibility shiftWork-burden shift is not responsibility shift. Human judgement, approval, escalation, communication and real-world action remain human-owned.
Productivity claimA productivity claim may be broad or vague. Work-burden shift asks what burden moved, what remained human-owned and what evidence supports that view.
Reproducibility proofPublic evidence can support initial capability evaluation, but protected-stage review is required before stronger method-transfer claims.

Example

Before, the person repeatedly rebuilds the AI work basis. After, shaped intelligence carries more of that two-sided work basis into review-ready form for the person to direct and approve.

Non-example

A faster draft that still requires the person to recreate the work basis, review status and resumption point from scratch.

Assessment signal

The visible burden areas include rebuilding the work basis, sequencing, drafting, tracking, dependency and risk framing, domain-practice and subject-context application, work-state and resumption handling, and communication preparation.

Read these as two connected sides: domain-practice and subject-context burden, plus work-state and resumption burden.
