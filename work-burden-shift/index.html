Work-burden shift

Work-burden shift is the before/after signal that more recurring work burden has moved from the responsible person to shaped intelligence.

Definition. Work-burden shift is the visible before/after change where more recurring work burden moves from the responsible person to shaped intelligence while judgement, approval and real-world action remain human-owned.

What is being tested

The relevant test is not whether an AI output is impressive. It is whether repeated work burden has shifted: reconstruction, sequencing, drafting, tracking, dependency and risk framing, project-state preservation, resumption and communication preparation.

Why the shift is bounded

Work-burden shift does not mean responsibility shift. The responsible person still owns judgement, approval, escalation, communication and real-world action. The shift is in recurring work burden, not in accountability.

Why it supports evidence

A before/after burden-shift view gives the concept a concrete public evidence signal. It lets a reviewer ask what changed, what was carried forward, what remained human-owned and what still requires protected-stage review.
