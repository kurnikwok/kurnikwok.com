Protected-stage discussion

Protected-stage discussion is the pathway for method-level review, collaboration or commercial discussion after public category understanding or public-stage evidence review is not enough.

Email projects@kurnikwok.com Review public papers first

Safe public explanation

The public site can explain the simple value: AI shaping gives AI a practice-guided, subject-aware work pattern and carries work state forward so complex work can be reviewed, resumed and reused.

Protected implementation layer

Protected discussion is for the exact shaping sequence, control structure, state-carrying schema, validation mechanics, route controls and domain-specific operating material.

Use this pathway when

  • You have read the relevant public page or paper and need implementation-level review.
  • The discussion would require method-level detail, protected operating material or non-public context.
  • The purpose is collaboration, client discussion, commercial discussion or a scoped capability review.

Do not use public material as

  • Implementation guidance.
  • Deployment assurance.
  • Reproducibility proof.
  • Product-performance assurance.
  • Method transfer or commercial-use rights.

Before protected material is reviewed

The public-stage boundary remains in place until protective purpose, confidentiality, ownership, scope and permitted use are agreed. Those conditions define what may be discussed, what may be relied on and what remains outside the discussion.

ConditionPurpose
Protective purposeWhy protected review is needed and what decision the discussion supports.
ConfidentialityHow non-public material, context and discussion content will be protected.
OwnershipWho owns existing material, protected method content and any discussion outputs.
ScopeWhich problem, domain, evidence question or collaboration question is in scope.
Permitted useWhat the recipient may and may not do with protected material or discussion outputs.

What to include when emailing

  • Which public page or paper you reviewed.
  • The reason public material is not enough for the next question.
  • The proposed discussion purpose and scope.
  • Whether the interest is review, collaboration, client discussion or commercial discussion.
  • Any confidentiality or ownership constraints that should be known before discussion.
