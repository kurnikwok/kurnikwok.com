Protected-stage discussion for scoped AI Shaping review

Use this route when a defined role-fit, collaboration, practice, capability, evidence or implementation question cannot be evaluated adequately from the public website and papers alone.

Email projects@kurnikwok.comCheck the public route first
Use this page only when

A defined question remains after public review

This is the canonical boundary source for deciding whether a question should remain public or move into minimum necessary protected review. Private codification does not itself warrant access or discussion.

A relevant work problem, sufficient public evidence and a defined purpose may justify considering a scoped protected-stage discussion.

Questions that may justify consideration

  • Whether selected AI Shaping principles or techniques are relevant to a defined work problem.
  • Whether a proposed capability fits the work object, recurring burden and human-authority boundary.
  • What practitioner guidance, architecture pattern or evaluation criterion is needed for a scoped decision.
  • How evidence limitations, rematerialisation or transfer-readiness should be assessed.
  • Whether a defined role-fit, advisory, collaboration, capability-development or implementation-review scope is appropriate.

Private material that may be relevant

  • Complete AI Shaping principle specifications, examples and anti-patterns.
  • Testable expectations and evaluation checks.
  • Selected shaping techniques, practices or architecture patterns.
  • Bootstrap, renewal, review or capability-readiness material.
  • Selected implementation correspondence needed for the defined question.

Only the minimum relevant material should be considered, and only after the protective conditions are agreed.

Safe public explanation

The public site and papers explain the category, core mechanism, evidence roles, current public instances, principles at a compact level and the public evaluation boundary. Use those sources first wherever they can answer the question.

What protected discussion does not promise

  • The complete private paper set or complete Practitioner Guide.
  • Shaped-control files, prompts, schemas or route controls.
  • Unrestricted method transfer or deployment assurance.
  • Commercial-use rights, licensing or a standard protected package.
  • Access merely because private principles or techniques exist.

Before protected material is reviewed

The public-stage boundary remains in place until the recipient, purpose, confidentiality, ownership, scope and permitted use are agreed.

Conditions before protected material is reviewed
ConditionPurpose
RecipientWho will receive or participate in the protected review.
PurposeWhy protected review is needed and what defined decision or question it supports.
ConfidentialityHow non-public material, context and discussion content will be protected.
OwnershipWho owns existing material, protected method content and any discussion outputs.
ScopeWhich role, work problem, capability, evidence or implementation question is included.
Permitted useWhat the recipient may and may not do with protected material or discussion outputs.

What to include when emailing

  • The public page or paper already reviewed.
  • The defined role, work problem or decision.
  • Why public material is insufficient for that specific question.
  • The proposed recipient, purpose and minimum requested scope.
  • Any confidentiality, ownership or permitted-use constraints.
