AI shaping

AI shaping is an operating-model discipline for turning general-purpose AI capability into productive AI work under human direction.

Definition. AI shaping is the operating-model discipline for turning broad, general-purpose AI capability into grounded, reviewable, repeatable and resumable productive AI work under human direction.

What the term addresses

AI systems can produce useful outputs without carrying the recurring burden of work. AI shaping starts from that gap. It asks how a work pattern can be structured so that the AI contribution is not a one-off answer, but a reusable, reviewable and resumable capability inside a real work domain.

How it fits the concept stack

AI shaping is the category-level discipline. AI-shaping intelligence is the reusable meta-capability that builds and renews the shaping pattern. Shaped intelligence is the domain-facing capability that carries recurring work burden. Productive AI work is the intended outcome. Work-burden shift is the visible before/after signal used to assess whether the operating model has changed.

Human authority boundary

AI shaping does not remove human responsibility. Direction, judgement, approval, escalation, disclosure, communication and real-world action remain human-owned. The point is to shift more of the reconstruction, sequencing, drafting, tracking, framing, resumption and continuity burden into shaped intelligence, without pretending that accountability has moved.
