Category definition paper

AI Shaping: Turning General-Purpose AI into Productive AI Work

Defines the category, concept stack, adjacent-category boundaries and protected-method limits.

Open DOI Zenodo record PDF

Abstract

This paper defines AI shaping as the operating-model discipline for turning broad general-purpose AI capability into productive AI work. It frames the central problem as the difference between useful AI output and work that is grounded, reviewable, repeatable and resumable under human direction. The paper introduces AI-shaping intelligence as the reusable meta-capability that builds, improves and renews its own shaping pattern, and shaped intelligence as the domain-facing reusable capability that carries more of the recurring work burden in a work domain. It explains work burden, work-burden shift, the concept stack, category fit, adjacent-category boundaries and protected-method limits. The paper supports public category understanding only. It does not provide capability evidence, implementation guidance, product specification, method transfer or deployment assurance. Where evidence review is warranted, the companion capability evidence paper should be used for the structured public evidence package under the public-stage and protected-stage limits described here.
