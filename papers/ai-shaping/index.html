AI Shaping: Turning General-Purpose AI into Productive AI Work

Defines AI shaping as the operating-model discipline for giving general-purpose AI a practice-guided, subject-aware work pattern and the work-state preservation and work-resumption logic needed for grounded, reviewable, repeatable and resumable productive AI work under human direction.

Abstract

This paper defines AI shaping as the operating-model discipline for turning broad general-purpose AI capability into productive AI work. It frames the central problem as the difference between useful AI output and work that is grounded, reviewable, repeatable and resumable under human direction.

The paper introduces AI-shaping intelligence as the reusable meta-capability that builds, improves and renews its own shaping pattern and uses that pattern to produce domain-facing shaped intelligence. It also defines shaped intelligence as the domain-facing reusable capability that carries more of the recurring work burden in a work domain. It explains work burden, work-burden shift, the concept stack, category fit, adjacent-category boundaries and protected-method limits.
