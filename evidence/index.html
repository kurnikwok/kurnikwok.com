Evidence overview

AI shaping evidence separates capability instances from public evaluation

Use this page to read the public evidence in brief and choose the right evidence route: AI-shaping intelligence evidence, project-managing shaped intelligence, the public evaluation map or the formal capability evidence paper.

Evidence architecture

Two instances, one public evidence route

The evidence layer separates two related capability instances. AI-shaping intelligence is the reusable shaping-capability instance: it renews the shaping pattern and produces shaped intelligence under human direction. Project-managing shaped intelligence is the current domain-facing shaped-intelligence instance: it carries project and BAU IT support domain-practice standards, subject-context reasoning, work-state preservation and work-resumption logic under human authority.

The public website can route and explain those roles. The formal public evidence source remains the capability evidence paper, and implementation-level correspondence remains protected-stage.

Evidence in brief

The public evidence route has two related but different capability instances. Project-managing shaped intelligence is the current domain-facing evidence instance: it shows visible work-burden shift in project and BAU IT support work. AI-shaping intelligence is the reusable shaping-capability instance: it builds, improves and renews shaped intelligence under human direction.

The diagrams below give a public-safe before/after view. They are orientation aids only. They do not disclose implementation mechanics, provide deployment assurance, prove reproducibility or authorise method transfer.

What would not be enough evidence?

Useful one-off AI output, a polished demo, a private implementation description by itself, or an illustrative transfer map without visible burden-shift evidence would not be enough. The public question is narrower: whether the available evidence shows reviewable work-burden shift under human authority, while leaving implementation-level review to protected-stage discussion.
