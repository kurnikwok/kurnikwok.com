Evidence overview

AI shaping evidence starts with visible work-burden shift

Public evidence asks whether the durable shaped-capability mechanism produces a visible operational effect: more two-sided recurring work burden carried by shaped intelligence while source basis and human authority remain explicit. Evidence strength depends on the bounded instance, not the diagram or wording alone.

Evidence architecture

A visible instance, an integrated publication system and a separate mediated evidence route

The evidence layer begins with web-developing shaped intelligence, a bounded website-specific direct-shaped instance that is readily inspectable at the observable-effect level. It then expands to publication co-evolution, which explains two separately governed paper-authoring instances and their integration with web-developing shaped intelligence. Separately, AI-shaping intelligence is the reusable shaping capability in the mediated-shaping approach; it developed vendor-evaluating shaped intelligence and extended that capability into project-managing shaped intelligence, the primary published domain-facing capability-evidence instance.

For professional evaluation, this sequence makes the connected work surface, evidence status and human-authority boundary visible without treating distinct capability instances as interchangeable.

The publication-system evidence has differentiated inspectability: the website and released published papers are publicly observable, while the private paper set is disclosed only at a bounded role-and-relationship level. The DOI-backed Capability Evidence paper remains the formal public source for the mediated project evidence route through AI-shaping intelligence. None of the direct-shaped publication instances evidences AI-shaping-intelligence domain transfer.

Evidence in brief

The four diagrams answer four progressively harder questions. Web-developing shaped intelligence makes the burden-shift mechanism easy to see. Publication co-evolution shows two paper-authoring instances plus their integration with the website instance. AI-shaping intelligence shows the reusable shaping capability. Project-managing shaped intelligence shows the more complex, formally published domain-facing evidence instance in project and BAU IT support work.

The diagrams give public-safe before/after views. They show observable capability relationships and burden shift without disclosing implementation mechanics, providing deployment assurance, proving reproducibility or authorising method transfer.

Evidence–plan boundary

Planned directions are not additional evidence roles

The three planned capability directions sit outside the current public evidence package. Naming a planned direction does not create a capability claim, evidence stream or transfer result.

What would not be enough evidence?

Useful one-off AI output, a polished demo, a private implementation description by itself, or an illustrative transfer map without visible burden-shift evidence would not be enough. The public question is narrower: whether the available evidence shows reviewable work-burden shift under human authority, while leaving implementation-level review to protected-stage discussion.
