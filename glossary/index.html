AI shaping glossary

Bite-sized definitions for the simple mechanism and concept stack used across the AI shaping papers and public pages.

The glossary explains how AI shaping moves from one-off prompting to reusable AI work patterns that carry context, source basis, domain-practice guidance, subject-specific context, review boundaries and work state under human direction.

AI shaping
The operating-model discipline for giving broad, general-purpose AI capability a practice-guided, subject-aware work pattern and work state so useful output can become grounded, reviewable, repeatable and resumable productive AI work under human direction. In simple terms, it moves from one-off prompting to reusable AI work patterns that carry context, standards, source basis, review rules and work state forward.
AI-shaping intelligence
The reusable meta-capability that builds, improves and renews its own shaping pattern so that shaped intelligence can carry more recurring work burden in a domain. In simple terms, it is the capability for using AI, under human direction, to improve the reusable work pattern that future AI work uses.
Shaped intelligence
The domain-facing reusable capability that carries more of the recurring work burden in a work domain, including domain-practice application and subject-context reasoning, while human direction, judgement and action remain explicit. In simple terms, it is AI using a reusable, domain-facing work pattern so the person does not rebuild the same context, standards, review boundaries and next-action logic every time.
Project-managing shaped intelligence
Project-managing shaped intelligence is the public capability-evidence instance of shaped intelligence in project and business-as-usual (BAU) IT support contexts, carrying recurring project and BAU IT support work burden while human authority remains explicit.
Domain-practice guidance
Guidance on how this kind of work should be done, including relevant work-practice standards, review expectations and operating patterns.
Domain-practice application
The use of relevant work-practice standards, review expectations and operating patterns inside shaped intelligence.
Subject-specific context
The matter-specific information the work depends on, such as the project, issue, stakeholder, support context, constraints or supplied facts.
Subject-context reasoning
The use of subject-specific project, issue, stakeholder or support-context information to frame reviewable work.
Work pattern
The shaped role, source basis, domain-practice guidance, subject-specific context, review boundaries and output expectations for a recurring kind of work. In simple terms, it is the reusable instruction-and-context bundle that helps AI handle the same kind of work consistently instead of starting from a fresh prompt each time.
Work state
The preserved position of the work: what happened, what changed, what is unresolved, what needs review and what happens next.
Productive AI work
AI-enabled work that is grounded, reviewable, repeatable and resumable under human direction.
Work burden
The recurring human load that must be carried before useful AI output can become productive AI work.
Work-burden shift
The visible before/after change where more recurring work burden moves from the responsible person to shaped intelligence while judgement, approval and real-world action remain human-owned.
Reconstruction
The repeated work of rebuilding the AI work basis before useful work can continue: context, source material, prior decisions, constraints, risks, review status and next action.
Resumption
Continuing work from a preserved reviewable state after a pause, handover, context loss or new information, instead of rebuilding the whole work basis again.
Context rebuilding
A plain-language version of reconstruction. In ordinary AI use, this often means writing a long or complex prompt to re-explain the work before the AI can help again.
Operating-model design
The broader discipline of arranging roles, controls, workflows, review boundaries and repeatable patterns so that work can be performed safely and consistently.
Grounded
Tied to source basis, context, review state and relevant constraints rather than detached from the work basis.
Reviewable
Structured so that human judgement, approval, disclosure and accountability remain explicit.
Repeatable
Able to recur without the person rebuilding the whole basis of the work each time.
Resumable
Able to continue after pause, handover or new information while preserving work state and next-action logic.
Public-stage
The public reading or evaluation stage, limited to category understanding or bounded capability evaluation without implementation reliance.
Public-stage material
Material that supports public category understanding or bounded public-stage capability evaluation, without providing method transfer or deployment assurance.
Protected-stage
The non-public stage required before implementation-level detail, method transfer or stronger assurance questions can be reviewed.
Protected-stage discussion
A non-public review context required for implementation-level detail, method transfer or stronger assurance questions.
