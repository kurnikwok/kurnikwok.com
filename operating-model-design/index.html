Operating-model design

Operating-model design is the broader frame for roles, controls, boundaries and repeatable work patterns in AI shaping.

Definition. Operating-model design is the broader discipline of arranging roles, controls, workflows, review boundaries and repeatable patterns so that work can be performed safely and consistently.

Why this is the parent frame

AI shaping is framed as an operating-model discipline because the problem is not only model capability. It is how capability is made usable inside a work pattern with continuity, controls, human review and clear responsibility.

What the design must preserve

A credible operating model must preserve source basis, decision authority, review route, disclosure boundary, state continuity and protected-method limits. Without those elements, the result can become a one-off output rather than productive AI work.

Relationship to the papers
