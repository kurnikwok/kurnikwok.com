About the work

About AI shaping: development context and credibility

AI shaping is the operating-model discipline for establishing shaped intelligence that carries a reusable and iteratively improvable domain work pattern and reusable work basis. The operational effect is two-sided work-burden shift; productive AI work is the outcome. This page explains the practical development context and author background only where it helps a reader assess credibility.

Credibility context

Why this development context matters

The work sits at the intersection of IT support, project delivery, product ownership, software development and operating-model design. That matters because AI shaping is presented as an operating-model discipline for shifting recurring work burden while source basis and the human-authority boundary remain explicit, not as a generic prompting trick.

How the work developed

  1. Investment-analysis roots and decision infrastructure

    The investment-analysis approach has earlier roots in postgraduate financial-management and MBA study and subsequent investment work. From July 2022, it was substantially developed alongside structured information and workflow support for investment analysis and portfolio review. The analytical work defined the evidence, decision-context and review needs; software development made the work operationally possible.

  2. Parallel AI-assisted development

    After conversational AI became available, it was adopted progressively across both streams: research and development across investment-analysis and portfolio-review work, and research, specification, coding, testing and workflow development for the supporting information system. The streams remained distinct in their work objects but functionally interdependent.

  3. From useful output to AI shaping

    Across both streams, highly useful AI analysis, research, specifications and code still left repeated human reconstruction of context, source basis, domain-practice standards, subject context, unresolved matters, work state and resumption. Solving that useful-output-to-productive-work gap became a prerequisite for advancing the underlying work and led to AI shaping. From 2025, vendor evaluation, device-upgrade project work and BAU IT support provided the route through which the shaping capability was further developed and made publicly inspectable: AI-shaping intelligence developed vendor-evaluating shaped intelligence and extended that shaped capability into project-managing shaped intelligence, the primary published domain-facing capability-evidence instance.

  4. Planned directions

    These intertwined origins now inform two formally distinct transfer-evaluation directions: stock-evaluating shaped intelligence and code-developing shaped intelligence. Possible portfolio-managing shaped intelligence remains a later superset extension dependent on first materialising and evaluating stock-evaluating shaped intelligence for one listed stock; the stock capability is deliberately not portfolio-aware. All remain planning context outside current public evidence.

Lessons from practice

Practice-derived principles

These principles distil recurring lessons from sustained practical development and application of AI shaping, source-grounded research into generative AI and human–AI work, and comparative experience across ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude and Copilot. ChatGPT has provided the principal experiential basis; use of the other platforms has been more limited and exploratory. This is a qualitative practice basis, not controlled platform benchmarking or a claim of universal applicability.

They guide practice rather than claim that every underlying idea is new, final or universally authoritative. Their proposed value lies in how the ideas are integrated around generative leverage, substantive work-burden shift, explicit work state, human direction and validation. They do not form part of the formal category definition or capability-evidence conclusion and remain open to refinement through further application, research and evaluation.

Work with AI’s nature

  • Align before shapingBegin with the model’s strongest available source-grounded account of established practice; introduce deviations only where purpose, evidence, context or human judgement justifies them.
  • Design for AI variabilityTreat generative-AI behaviour as probabilistic, changeable and platform-dependent; use AI shaping only where expected benefit justifies continuing review, testing, renewal and platform-specific validation.
  • Preserve AI leverageDirect and constrain work without unnecessarily suppressing the breadth, depth, synthesis and adaptation that make generative AI valuable.

Structure human–AI work

  • Bound AI workBound complex AI work through purpose-, role-, source- and stage-specific units; preserve continuity through explicit state, review and handoff rather than expecting one execution to carry the whole problem.
  • Maintain human directionMaintain active human direction over purpose, priorities, boundaries, trade-offs, stopping conditions and continuation while AI carries work within that direction.
  • Shift suitable work to AIOnce purpose, source basis, boundaries, authority and review conditions are established, shift suitable work to AI while retaining human judgement, review, approval, communication, decisions and real-world action.

Assure and renew the work

  • Validate before continuingTreat AI-carried work as provisional; validate it against purpose, sources, constraints, acceptance conditions and prior state, then remediate, revalidate, redirect or stop before consequential continuation.

The groups are for orientation. The principles can recur, interact and constrain one another throughout a work cycle; they are not a mandatory seven-step process.

One bounded application is web-developing shaped intelligence, established for this website through the direct-shaping approach. In that work, the seven principles operate together as recurring design and assurance guidance rather than as a sequential method.

A fuller practitioner-facing body of principle specifications, architecture patterns, techniques, practices, processes, examples, anti-patterns and evaluation checks is being codified and tested through the private paper set. This remains developing private material rather than final, universal or independently validated doctrine, and it is not a public implementation package.

The publication system uses separate private-set paper-authoring shaped intelligence and published-set paper-authoring shaped intelligence, alongside web-developing shaped intelligence. Their bounded roles and integration are explained on Publication co-evolution; the private papers, controls and operating mechanics remain non-public.

Selected related foundations

These selected sources support underlying concerns in human–AI interaction, AI risk management, decomposition, oversight, evaluation and principle-based codification. They do not endorse AI Shaping or imply that the principles are wholly derived from, equivalent to or validated by any cited source.

  • Guidelines for Human-AI Interaction (Microsoft Research, CHI 2019) — interaction expectations, correction, explanation and human oversight.
  • Artificial Intelligence Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0) (NIST, 2023) — lifecycle governance, mapping, measurement and risk management.
  • Artificial Intelligence Risk Management Framework: Generative Artificial Intelligence Profile (NIST, 2024) — generative-AI-specific risks, evaluation and management actions.
  • Principles of Mixed-Initiative User Interfaces (Eric Horvitz, CHI 1999) — allocation and coordination between human and automated contribution.
  • AI Chains: Transparent and Controllable Human-AI Interaction by Chaining Large Language Model Prompts (Wu, Terry and Cai, CHI 2022) — decomposition and controllable multi-step AI work.
  • Building effective agents (Anthropic, 2024) — simple composable workflows, routing, evaluator–optimizer loops and human checkpoints.
  • A practical guide to building agents (OpenAI) — use-case selection, orchestration, guardrails, evaluation and human intervention.
  • PMBOK® Guide—Eighth Edition (Project Management Institute) and PRINCE2 Project Management Foundation (Version 7) (PeopleCert) — structural benchmarks for concise principle codification only, not substantive AI Shaping authority.
Practitioner position

From rebuilding the work to directing it

I direct, review and decide. Shaped intelligence carries more of the recurring work.

Since July 2022, I have substantially developed this decision and software work from largely manual analysis, specification, coding and workflow development through ordinary conversational-AI use into shaped-intelligence capabilities. The practical shift is not the disappearance of human work or responsibility: I establish direction, challenge and review the work, approve what should proceed and decide what happens next.

This shift is publicly inspectable in website development and addressed through the published project-managing evidence instance. Investment evaluation and code development remain distinct planned transfer-evaluation directions; portfolio management remains a possible later extension.

The practical problem

Useful AI output is not the same as productive AI work that is grounded, practice-guided, subject-aware, reviewable, repeatable and resumable under human direction in real operating conditions.

AI shaping addresses the repeated work burden by shaping the work pattern and arranging a reusable work basis. Shaped intelligence carries more of the domain-practice and subject-context burden, and more of the work-state and resumption burden, so work can continue rather than restart while judgement, approval and real-world action remain human-owned.

From first wedge to domain extension and transfer

The development was not a straight jump from ordinary AI use to project-managing shaped intelligence. Early direct project-managing attempts in this instance made clear that the work needed a reusable shaping foundation, so the demonstrated evidence sequence used the mediated-shaping approach through AI-shaping intelligence: the reusable shaping capability behind the later shaped-intelligence instances.

The first successful wedge was vendor-evaluating shaped intelligence, which carried the burden of parsing vendor material, normalising options, assessing cost-benefit implications, framing risks and preparing decision material under human review. That shaped capability then extended into project-managing shaped intelligence because vendor evaluation was a subset of the broader project-management work pattern.

Domain extension means widening shaped intelligence within a related work family, where the earlier shaped capability becomes a subset of a broader shaped capability. Vendor-evaluating shaped intelligence to project-managing shaped intelligence is the current public example.

Domain transfer means target-domain rematerialisation: evaluating whether the shaping pattern can be developed against the conditions of a different work domain rather than assuming simple portability.

Project-managing shaped intelligence is the primary published domain-facing capability-evidence instance. Its significance is not that AI replaces human authority, judgement or action. Its significance is that more recurring project and BAU IT support work burden can be carried by shaped intelligence, while the responsible person directs, reviews, corrects, approves, decides, communicates and acts.

In practical terms, shaped intelligence carries more of both burden sides that the responsible person would otherwise rebuild across recurring project work. Source basis remains visible and the human-authority boundary remains intact. The public evidence is framed around that shift.

Current public evidence

The demonstrated mediated approach is domain-bounded rather than one all-purpose assistant established in a single step. A functioning AI-shaping intelligence developed vendor-evaluating shaped intelligence and then extended that shaped capability into broader project-managing shaped intelligence. Project-managing shaped intelligence remains the primary published domain-facing capability-evidence instance. A bounded, more readily inspectable website-specific direct-shaped example is available through web-developing shaped intelligence. A further direct-shaped evidence view is available through Publication co-evolution, which distinguishes the two paper-authoring instances and their integration with the website instance.

Future capability functions and route dependencies are kept separate on Planned capability directions. The code-development rationale is also connected to the bounded practitioner position on Specification-led vs Iterative AI work.
