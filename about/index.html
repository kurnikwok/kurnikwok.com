About the work

About AI shaping: development context and credibility

AI shaping is an operating-model discipline centred on shifting more two-sided recurring work burden to shaped intelligence so productive AI work can be grounded, practice-guided, subject-aware, reviewable, repeatable and resumable under human direction. This page explains the practical development context and the author background only where it helps a reader assess credibility.

Credibility context

Why this development context matters

The work sits at the intersection of IT support, project delivery, product ownership, software development and operating-model design. That matters because AI shaping is presented as an operating-model discipline for shifting recurring work burden while source basis and the human-authority boundary remain explicit, not as a generic prompting trick.

How the work developed

The work began with a practical investment decision-workflow problem: how to preserve why investment decisions were made, what was known at the time, what constraints applied, what changed afterwards and what should improve next time. The system was built around governed decision-making, evidence-linked reasoning, reviewable decision ownership and learning over time.

As general-purpose AI became more useful in the development process, the challenge changed. Better prompts helped, but prompt-by-prompt control still left too much recurring work with the person.

The practical need became a reusable shaping capability that could develop and improve the work pattern under human direction, then use that pattern to develop domain-facing shaped intelligence. The point was not only to produce outputs, but to shift more recurring work burden into a reusable work capability.

That development path produced AI shaping as an operating-model discipline and a capability architecture in which shaped intelligence may be developed directly or through AI-shaping intelligence. The demonstrated development history described here is mediated shaping: AI-shaping intelligence is the reusable shaping capability that develops, improves and renews its own shaping pattern and uses that pattern to develop domain-facing shaped intelligence. Shaped intelligence is the reusable domain-facing capability that carries more recurring work burden under human direction.

The practical problem

Useful AI output is not the same as productive AI work that is grounded, practice-guided, subject-aware, reviewable, repeatable and resumable under human direction in real operating conditions.

AI shaping addresses the repeated work burden by shaping the work pattern and arranging a reusable work basis. Shaped intelligence carries more of the domain-practice and subject-context burden, and more of the work-state and resumption burden, so work can continue rather than restart while human review, approval and action remain human-owned.

From first wedge to domain extension and transfer

The path was not a straight jump from ordinary AI use to project-managing shaped intelligence. Early direct project-managing attempts made clear that this work needed a stronger foundation, so the demonstrated route became mediated shaping through AI-shaping intelligence: the reusable shaping capability behind the later shaped-intelligence instances.

The first successful wedge was vendor-evaluating shaped intelligence, which carried the burden of parsing vendor material, normalising options, assessing cost-benefit implications, framing risks and preparing decision material under human review. That shaped capability then extended into project-managing shaped intelligence because vendor evaluation was a subset of the broader project-management work pattern.

Domain extension means widening shaped intelligence within a related work family, where the earlier shaped capability becomes a subset of a broader shaped capability. Vendor-evaluating shaped intelligence to project-managing shaped intelligence is the current public example.

Domain transfer means target-domain rematerialisation: evaluating whether the shaping pattern can be developed against the conditions of a different work domain rather than assuming simple portability. Investment-evaluating shaped intelligence is the planned next transfer-evaluation direction into investment portfolio management, not a current public capability-evidence claim.

Project-managing shaped intelligence is the primary published domain-facing capability-evidence instance. Its significance is not that AI replaces human authority, judgement or action. Its significance is that more recurring project and BAU IT support work burden can be carried by shaped intelligence, while the responsible person directs, reviews, corrects, approves, decides, communicates and acts.

In practical terms, shaped intelligence carries more of the domain-practice and subject-context burden, and more of the work-state and resumption burden, that the responsible person would otherwise rebuild across recurring project work. Source basis remains visible and the human-authority boundary remains intact. The public evidence is framed around that shift.

Broader design direction

Primary published domain-facing capability-evidence instance

The stronger demonstrated path is domain-bounded rather than one all-purpose assistant in a single step. A functioning AI-shaping intelligence developed vendor-evaluating shaped intelligence and then extended that shaped capability into broader project-managing shaped intelligence. Project-managing shaped intelligence remains the primary published domain-facing capability-evidence instance. A simpler website-specific example is available through web-developing shaped intelligence.

Planned transfer-then-extension evaluation

The planned next evaluation is target-domain rematerialisation into investment-evaluating shaped intelligence. If that transfer is successfully materialised and evidenced, portfolio-managing shaped intelligence is a possible later domain extension within the investment portfolio management work family.

These are planned evaluation directions, not current public capability-evidence claims. They do not imply self-authorised trade execution or replacement of the human decision-maker.
