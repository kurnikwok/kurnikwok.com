AI Shaping practice

AI Shaping principles, lifecycle and techniques

AI shaping is supported by established practice-derived principles, a complete capability lifecycle and reusable practitioner techniques. This public view explains their roles and relationships without transferring the protected implementation method.

Principles govern what must remain true. The lifecycle governs when work, decisions and transitions occur. Techniques govern how particular recurring work is performed.

Practice architecture

Principles → Lifecycle → Techniques

The hierarchy is strategic, not merely a reading convention. Principles constrain the whole practice. The lifecycle organises the capability across qualification, establishment, operation, improvement, renewal and retirement. Techniques are reusable ways of working that support one phase, several phases or recurring work across the lifecycle.

A technique can span phases without becoming more strategic than the lifecycle. A concise public formulation does not replace the complete private specification.

Highest strategic layer

AI Shaping principles

The seven principles are established within the current AI Shaping body of work. They are practice-derived, evidence-sensitive and iteratively improvable under human direction. They are not an external standard, universal doctrine, complete body of knowledge, mandatory seven-step process or independent implementation authority.

Work with AI’s nature

  • Align before shapingBegin with the model’s strongest available source-grounded account of established practice; introduce deviations only where purpose, evidence, context or human judgement justifies them.
  • Design for AI variabilityTreat generative-AI behaviour as probabilistic, changeable and platform-dependent; use AI shaping only where expected benefit justifies continuing review, testing, renewal and platform-specific validation.
  • Preserve AI leverageDirect and constrain work without unnecessarily suppressing the breadth, depth, synthesis and adaptation that make generative AI valuable.

Structure human–AI work

  • Bound AI workBound complex AI work through purpose-, role-, source- and stage-specific units; preserve continuity through explicit state, review and handoff rather than expecting one execution to carry the whole problem.
  • Maintain human directionMaintain active human direction over purpose, priorities, boundaries, trade-offs, stopping conditions and continuation while AI carries work within that direction.
  • Shift suitable work to AIOnce purpose, source basis, boundaries, authority and review conditions are established, shift suitable work to AI while retaining human judgement, review, approval, communication, decisions and real-world action.

Assure and renew the work

  • Validate before continuingTreat AI-carried work as provisional; validate it against purpose, sources, constraints, acceptance conditions and prior state, then remediate, revalidate, redirect or stop before consequential continuation.

The groups are memory aids. The principles recur, interact and constrain one another throughout the lifecycle.

Complete capability lifecycle

Lifecycle

The lifecycle covers the whole capability over time, not only establishment. The phases are ordered for orientation and decision clarity, but proportionate iteration remains possible under human direction.

  1. Qualify the opportunity

    Test whether recurring work has stable-enough structure and sufficient value to justify shaping.

  2. Bound the capability

    Define the work, users, authority, sources, recurring burden, exclusions, consequence and intended value.

  3. Select the shaping approach

    Choose the least elaborate reliable direct or mediated architecture.

  4. Establish and materialise

    Create controls, source and authority basis, work pattern, current state, output, review and resumption basis.

  5. Validate and accept

    Test fitness, remediate findings and obtain bounded human acceptance before consequential continuation.

  6. Operate and reuse

    Apply the accepted capability across changing subjects or successive revisions while preserving state and decisions.

  7. Maintain and iteratively improve

    Correct defects and improve the pattern, source handling, validation, reviewability and state.

  8. Adapt or materially renew

    Respond proportionately to major platform, source, regulatory, domain, authority or repeated-failure change.

  9. Evaluate extension or transfer

    Test broader related work or a different domain under an explicit new boundary.

  10. Retire, supersede or archive

    Cease reliance responsibly and preserve required history, state and residual obligations.

Improvement claim boundary: improvability by design is not the same as actual improvement demonstrated through accepted revision. A measured-improvement claim additionally requires defined measures and suitable evidence.

Selected public view

Techniques

Techniques are reusable operational ways of working. The selection below is explicitly non-exhaustive and gives names and public-safe purposes only. A technique may support one phase, span several phases or recur across the lifecycle.

Selected AI Shaping techniques and public-safe purposes
TechniquePublic-safe purpose
Separate shaping, capability and subjectPrevent reusable shaping controls, domain work patterns and current subject state from becoming inseparable.
Bound the reuse domainDefine the work object, work family, users, recurring burden, exclusions and stop rules.
Identify the owning surfaceKeep one controlling location for each rule, status, claim or complete shared definition.
Define compatibility contractsMake identity, version, target, source and migration conditions explicit before relying on compatibility.
Use substitution testingReplace a bounded subject, configuration or platform and observe what must remain stable or change.
Preserve state and resumptionCarry the current basis, decisions, unresolved matters, review status and next route across work cycles.
Validate, remediate and revalidateSeparate production from evaluation and do not continue with unresolved material findings.
Change-impact reconciliationIdentify direct, indirect and cross-surface consequences before or during owner-level change resolution.
Control propagationDeliberately transfer an accepted owner-level change into separately authorised dependent surfaces.

Change-impact reconciliation identifies and evaluates consequences. Control propagation governs the deliberate transfer of an accepted change. Neither technique authorises an uncontrolled “update everywhere”.

Public conceptual view

What this page makes visible

The public site can show principle names and compact statements, compressed lifecycle phases, selected technique names and purposes, observable work-burden shift, source basis and the human-authority boundary.

This is enough to understand the practice architecture, recognise relevance and decide whether deeper protected discussion is justified.

Protected machinery

What remains private

Complete procedures, detailed gates, decision rules, readiness tests, prompts, templates, schemas, validation machinery, continuity and recovery mechanisms, sensitive records and sufficient material to reproduce or independently operate the method remain private or protected.
