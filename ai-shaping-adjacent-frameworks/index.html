AI shaping in context: agents, governance, workflow redesign and productivity evidence

Use this page when the question is not only “How is AI shaping different from prompts or agents?” but “Where does AI shaping sit beside current AI agent, governance, workflow-redesign and productivity-evidence language?”

In brief: adjacent frameworks describe important parts of the AI landscape. Agent guides focus on orchestration and tool use. Governance frameworks focus on risk, accountability and trustworthy use. Enterprise-adoption research focuses on workflow redesign and organisational uptake. Productivity studies test whether AI improves measured task outcomes. AI shaping sits beside those categories as the operating-model discipline for carrying the reusable work basis into productive AI work under human authority.

Adjacent source families and the AI shaping position

Read this as a positioning map. Each adjacent source family answers a valid question. AI shaping answers a narrower work-pattern question: can both sides of recurring work burden — domain-practice and subject-context burden, plus work-state and resumption burden — carry forward so AI-enabled output becomes productive work under human authority?

Reader test

When this context matters

  • The organisation already has prompts, copilots, agents or workflow tools, but people still rebuild the work basis repeatedly.
  • The governance problem is partly a work-design problem: reviewers cannot see source basis, unresolved matters, review state or human-owned next action clearly enough.
  • The adoption problem is not tool access alone; it is the absence of a stable, reviewable and resumable work pattern.
  • The productivity question needs to avoid generic AI-uplift claims and instead test a bounded burden-shift signal in a defined work setting.

What this context page is not

This context page is not a full literature review, an implementation guide or a claim that AI shaping is superior to adjacent frameworks. Its job is to show where AI shaping fits and then route formal readers to the papers.
