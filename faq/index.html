AI shaping FAQ

Short answers to common questions about AI shaping, AI-shaping intelligence, shaped intelligence, productive AI work, work-burden shift and the public-stage boundary.

What is AI shaping?

AI shaping is the operating-model discipline for turning general-purpose AI capability into grounded, reviewable, repeatable and resumable productive AI work under human direction.

Is AI shaping the same as prompt engineering?

No. Prompt engineering can improve an individual request or output. AI shaping is broader: it concerns the recurring work pattern, source basis, review route, continuity, boundaries and human authority around productive AI work.

What is AI-shaping intelligence?

AI-shaping intelligence is the reusable meta-capability that builds, improves and renews its own shaping pattern so that shaped intelligence can carry more recurring work burden in a domain.

What is shaped intelligence?

Shaped intelligence is the domain-facing reusable capability that carries more of the recurring work burden in a work domain while human direction, judgement and real-world action remain explicit.

What is project-managing shaped intelligence?

Project-managing shaped intelligence is the public evidence instance of shaped intelligence in project and business-as-usual IT support contexts. It makes domain-practice application, subject-context reasoning, project-state preservation and resumption explicit.

What is productive AI work?

Productive AI work is AI-enabled work that is grounded, reviewable, repeatable and resumable under human direction. It is a higher threshold than useful AI output.

What does work-burden shift mean?

Work-burden shift is the visible before/after change where more recurring work burden moves from the responsible person to shaped intelligence while judgement, approval and real-world action remain human-owned.

Does work-burden shift mean responsibility shift?

No. The burden that shifts is recurring work such as reconstruction, sequencing, drafting, tracking, framing and resumption. Responsibility, approval, escalation, communication and real-world action remain human-owned.

Which paper should I read first?

Read the category definition paper first if you need the definition, concept stack and boundaries. Read the capability evidence paper first if you need the bounded public evidence instance and work-burden shift test.

Do the papers prove deployment readiness?

No. The public release supports category understanding and bounded public-stage capability evaluation. It does not provide deployment assurance, product-performance assurance or reproducibility proof.

Can someone implement the method from the public papers?

No. The public pages and papers do not provide implementation guidance, method transfer or commercial use rights. Implementation-level review requires protected-stage discussion.

Why are the papers dense?

They are dense because they are doing category-definition and bounded evidence work, not casual marketing explanation. The website provides shorter concept pages so the papers can remain precise and defensible.

Why not just call this AI productivity?

AI productivity is too broad for the specific claim. AI shaping focuses on operating-model design, human authority, shaped capability and whether recurring work burden can shift while accountability remains human-owned.
