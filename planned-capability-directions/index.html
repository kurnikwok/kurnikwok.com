Plan

Planned capability directions for shaped intelligence

The current public plan explains what three planned shaped-intelligence capabilities would carry, why the investment direction is deliberately two-step and why code development is a separate transfer-evaluation direction. All remain planning context outside current evidence.

Planning context

Shared public claim status, distinct functions and route relationships

Investment-evaluating shaped intelligence, portfolio-managing shaped intelligence and code-developing shaped intelligence share the same public claim status: planning context outside the current public capability-evidence package. Their work objects, functions, dependencies and evaluation routes differ, and naming or illustrating a direction does not create evidence or rank maturity.

The intended practical direction is the same: move from repeatedly rebuilding the work manually to directing shaped intelligence that can carry more of the recurring domain burden. That remains an evaluation objective, not a current capability result.

Investment direction

Why investment evaluation comes before portfolio management

The underlying development work began in July 2022 with a structured information system and workflow for personal investment evaluation and portfolio review. It organised monitored assets, holdings, transactions, evidence, decision context, portfolio outcomes, governance and review so recurring decisions could be made more consistently, reviewably and transparently.

For planned AI-shaping evaluation, that broader problem is separated into two bounded work objects. Investment-evaluating shaped intelligence would first carry the recurring work needed to form and review an investment decision basis. Portfolio-managing shaped intelligence would be a later, broader capability carrying portfolio state and relationships among holdings, transactions, constraints, exposures, outcomes and future decisions. Portfolio management is therefore not merely investment evaluation repeated more often.

The sequence uses the same structural hypothesis as the demonstrated vendor-evaluating-to-project-managing progression: first establish a bounded evaluating capability, then assess whether it can extend into the broader managing capability. That similarity is planning rationale only; it does not establish that the investment-domain route will succeed. The public development chronology appears on About.

Code-development direction

Why code development is a separate transfer evaluation

The software-development path progressed from manual system and workflow development into exploratory AI use, then AI-assisted research, specification, coding and structured workflow design. The planned question is not whether AI can generate code. It is whether AI-shaping intelligence can carry more of the recurring burden across changing requirements, architecture, implementation, testing, defect handling, review and resumption.

For uncertain or evolving code-development work, this site treats iterative AI work as the stronger default while recognising that iteration alone can leave continuity, review-state and resumption burden with the person. The bounded development position is explained on Specification-led vs Iterative AI work. The private application stack and implementation mechanics remain outside the public plan.

How the route relationships differ

Investment-evaluating and code-developing shaped intelligence are separate prospective cross-domain transfer-evaluation directions. Portfolio-managing shaped intelligence is a possible later extension whose evaluation depends on first materialising and evaluating investment-evaluating shaped intelligence. All three remain planning context; route logic and dependency do not create current capability evidence.

What changes a direction's status

A planned direction should not become a public capability claim until the work domain is bounded, the shaping route is established, shaped intelligence is materially developed, repeated work-burden shift is evaluated and a public-safe evidence object supports the claim.

Public planning boundary

Planning context only: these directions are not current capability evidence, implementation commitments, product announcements or authority to rely on a capability. Investment-related directions do not provide financial advice or authority to act; the code-development direction does not provide implementation guidance or software assurance.
