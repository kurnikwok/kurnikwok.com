Planned capability directions for shaped intelligence

The current public plan contains three peer shaped-intelligence directions. They are described at the work-problem and evaluation-question level without turning planning context into capability evidence or disclosing private implementation material.

Planning context

Three peer planned capability directions

Investment-evaluating shaped intelligence, portfolio-managing shaped intelligence and code-developing shaped intelligence have the same public planning status. None is part of the current public capability-evidence package. Different route relationships or the presence of a diagram do not rank their maturity.

Planned directions at a glance

Planned capability directionRecurring work focusPublic evaluation questionDomain-specific public limit
Investment-evaluating shaped intelligenceInvestment-evaluation work that must preserve decision basis, evidence, assumptions, unresolved questions, review context and next action.Can shaped intelligence carry more recurring investment-evaluation burden while decisions and real-world action remain human-owned?No financial advice or authority to act.
Portfolio-managing shaped intelligencePortfolio-management work that must preserve holdings context, constraints, dependencies, outcomes, review state and continuation logic.Can shaped intelligence carry more recurring portfolio-management burden while portfolio decisions and real-world action remain human-owned?No portfolio-management assurance or financial advice.
Code-developing shaped intelligenceCode-development work across research, specification, implementation, testing, defect handling, work state and resumption.Can shaped intelligence carry more recurring code-development burden while architecture, review, approval and deployment action remain human-owned?No implementation guidance or software assurance.

Equal status does not require identical routes

Investment-evaluating and code-developing shaped intelligence may be evaluated as cross-domain transfer directions. Portfolio-managing shaped intelligence may test a related-work-family extension relationship. These route questions are evaluated separately and do not create a hierarchy among the three public planning directions.

What changes a direction's status

A planned direction should not become a public capability claim until the work domain is bounded, the shaping route is established, shaped intelligence is materially developed, repeated work-burden shift is evaluated and a public-safe evidence object supports the claim.

Public planning boundary

Planning context only: these directions are not current capability evidence, implementation commitments, product announcements or authority to rely on a capability. Investment-related directions do not provide financial advice or authority to act; code-development direction does not provide implementation guidance or software assurance.
